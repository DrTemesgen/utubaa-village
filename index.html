A village where every pillar is a life that held others up

Utubaa is the pillar at the heart of the home. Utubaa Village is a digital village of such pillars — one raised for every verified, unnoticed Ethiopian whose life's work carried others: the teacher, the nurse, the farmer, the elder, the builder no one filmed.

Built in the spirit of medemer — nothing is complete alone; each person adds what they have to the whole.

How a pillar is earnedThe journey

Not fame. Not money. Not politics. Evidence.

No one enters the Village by being famous, by paying, or by winning a poll. A person is nominated only by people who lived alongside their work — a student, a patient, a neighbor, a colleague — and every claim must come with evidence. Nothing unverified is ever written into the record.

Five rules, written before the first pillar rises

The founding charter locks these in — so the bar can never move to fill a ceremony.

  1. Money never touches merit.
    The body that holds funds is constitutionally barred from choosing laureates.
  2. The award may be withheld.
    If no dossier meets the bar, no award is given that year — with the reasons published.
  3. AI screens, humans judge.
    Intelligent systems organize and cross-check evidence; every consequential judgment is made and signed by a named human being.
  4. The process itself is public.
    Criteria, scoring, acceptance rates, and audits are published. Only people's private files are sealed.
  5. The same rules at every tier.
    Village, nation, continent, world — one standard.

How a pillar is earned

Seven stages stand between a nomination and a pillar in the Village.

  1. Nomination — by three close witnesses, in any Ethiopian language, written or spoken.
  2. Screening — AI checks completeness and independence; a named officer signs every decision.
  3. Consent — nothing proceeds without the nominee's written agreement.
  4. Cross-verification — every claim is broken down and tested against independent evidence.
  5. Field verification — trained verifiers travel to the place of the work and speak with the people it touched.
  6. Adjudication — an independent council decides in an AI-free room, and may award no one.
  7. The pillar rises — the verified story is published, and a virtual pillar joins the Village — in time, in full 360°.

Why this must exist

In the only published integrity study of an established national awards industry, 61% of award schemes required no supporting evidence and 43% had a single judging stage. Children grow up knowing the names of the famous, not the names of the good. Utubaa Village exists so that a nation's gratitude finally lands where it is owed — and so the next generation can walk among pillars and choose whom to become.
