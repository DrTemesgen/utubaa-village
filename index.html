A village where every pillar is a life that held others up

Utubaa is the pillar at the heart of the home. Utubaa Village is a digital village of such pillars — one raised for every verified, unnoticed Ethiopian whose life's work carried others: the teacher, the nurse, the farmer, the elder, the builder no one filmed.

Built in the spirit of medemer — nothing is complete alone; each person adds what they have to the whole.

Verified Evidence.

No one enters the Village by being famous, by paying, or by winning a poll. A person is nominated only by people who lived alongside their work — a student, a patient, a neighbour, a colleague — and every claim must come with evidence. Nothing unverified is ever written into the record.

10 years

of verified, sustained contribution — the work must already be done, never merely promised.

7 close witnesses

at least seven people who knew the work, each with a signed or recorded account in their own words.

7 evidences

at least seven independent evidence items, from two or more classes; core claims verified in person.

Five rules, written before the first pillar rises

The founding charter locks these in — so the bar can never move to fill a ceremony.

  1. Money never touches merit.
    The body that holds funds is barred from choosing laureates.
  2. The award may be withheld.
    If no one meets the bar, no award is given that year — with the reasons published.
  3. AI screens, humans judge.
    Systems organize and cross-check; every consequential judgment is signed by a named human.
  4. The process itself is public.
    Criteria, scoring, and audits are published. Only people's private files are sealed.
  5. The same rules at every tier.
    Village, nation, continent, world — one standard.
